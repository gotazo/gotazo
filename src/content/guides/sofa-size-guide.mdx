- Walking space  
- Comfort  
- Overall room layout

## Why Sofa Size Matters (Most People Ignore This)

Small living rooms need **balance**, not just furniture.

A wrong-sized sofa can:
- Block movement  
- Make the room feel cramped  
- Ruin layout flow  

The right sofa:
- Keeps the space open  
- Improves comfort  
- Makes your room look bigger

## Ideal Sofa Width for Small Living Rooms

Width is the **most important factor**.

| Sofa Type | Ideal Width |
|----------|------------|
| Compact 2-Seater | 45 – 60 inches |
| Standard 2-Seater | 55 – 65 inches |
| Compact 3-Seater | 65 – 72 inches |

👉 If your wall is **8–9 feet**, go with **55–65 inches**.

👉 For tighter rooms → choose a **2-seater sofa**

## Ideal Sofa Depth (CRITICAL for Small Rooms)

Depth decides how much **walking space you lose**.

- Compact sofas → **26–30 inches**
- Standard sofas → **30–36 inches**

👉 Sweet spot: **28–32 inches**

⚠️ Deep sofas are the #1 mistake in small homes.

---

## Ideal Sofa Height

Height affects **visual space perception**.

- Low profile → **26–30 inches**
- Standard → **30–36 inches**

👉 Lower sofas = room looks bigger

---

## 📏 Measure Your Room (Before Buying ANY Sofa)

Always measure:

- Wall width  
- Walking clearance  
- Door entry size  
- Distance to table  

👉 Minimum walking space: **24–30 inches**
